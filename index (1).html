Canada’s Role in Space Today

When people think about space exploration, they imagine powerful nations racing to be first. Canada never tried to dominate space. Instead, Canada focused on contribution over competition. Today, we explore how Canada became essential in space through robotics, partnerships, and peaceful leadership.

1. Leadership in Space Robotics

Canada’s most important contribution to space exploration is robotics. The Canadarm first flew in 1981 and allowed astronauts to safely deploy satellites and move heavy equipment in orbit.

Canadarm2 and Dextre later became essential to building and maintaining the International Space Station. Canada is now developing Canadarm3 for the Lunar Gateway, continuing this legacy into deep space.

2. International Partnerships

Canada believes space exploration works best through cooperation. By partnering with NASA and other agencies, Canada contributes specialized technology in exchange for scientific access.

Canada provided critical instruments for the James Webb Space Telescope and earned an astronaut seat on Artemis II, sending Jeremy Hansen around the Moon.

3. Peaceful Leadership & Canadian Values

Canada supports the peaceful use of space and signed the Artemis Accords to promote transparency, sustainability, and responsible exploration.

Space technology also benefits Canadians on Earth through climate research, disaster response, and scientific innovation.
